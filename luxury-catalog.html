Section 02Studio Profile

La Maison is a Hyderabad-based interior furnishings studio operating since 2011. We design, source, and specify furniture for residential and commercial projects across India and Southeast Asia.

Our approach is systematic: every piece is documented with material composition, dimensional tolerances, and lead times before it enters the collection.

We work exclusively with certified artisan studios and FSC-compliant timber suppliers. Each product undergoes a three-stage quality review — prototype, production sample, and final inspection.

Trade accounts receive access to our full specification library, CAD blocks, and material swatch sets upon registration.

Section 03Design Principles
01
Material honesty

Every surface reveals its origin. We do not simulate wood grain on polymer, nor apply metallic finishes to base alloys without disclosure in the specification sheet.

02
Dimensional clarity

All published measurements include tolerance bands. Nominal dimensions are measured at finished state, not rough assembly. Custom sizing available on request with revised lead times.

03
Longevity over trend

Collection pieces are designed for a minimum 15-year service life under normal residential use. We retire styles only when material supply chains become unsustainable.

04
Traceable sourcing

Each SKU links to a supplier record. Clients may request chain-of-custody documentation for timber, stone, and metal components at the point of order.

Our Story — Est. 2011

A studio built on slow craft

La Maison began in a small workshop in Hyderabad with a single belief: furniture should be made slowly, by skilled hands, from materials that age with grace.

Today we partner with twelve artisan studios across India, Portugal, and Italy — each chosen for a specific craft tradition.

We don't follow seasons. We follow materials — what the forest offers, what the kiln produces, what the loom weaves. Every piece in this catalog was made to be passed down, not replaced.

How Every Piece Is Made

From raw material to your home — four stages of intentional craft.

1
Material selection

We visit forests, quarries, and weaving cooperatives personally. Only batches that meet our grain, colour, and structural standards enter production.

2
Prototype & refinement

Each new design spends 6–8 weeks in prototype. Joints are stress-tested, finishes are aged artificially, and proportions are adjusted by hand.

3
Artisan production

Pieces are made in small batches of 12–24 units. No assembly lines — one craftsperson oversees each piece from start to finish.

4
Final inspection & delivery

Every item is photographed, numbered, and wrapped in recycled cotton. White-glove delivery is included for orders above ₹50,000.

Materials We Trust
Four foundations of every La Maison piece.
European White Oak

Kiln-dried, FSC-certified. We use only quarter-sawn boards for structural pieces — tighter grain, less movement over decades.

Carrara Marble

Honed, not polished — so it feels warm under hand. Each top is sealed with a food-safe impregnator.

Natural Rattan & Cane

Harvested from managed plantations in Kerala. Borax-treated for longevity, left unfinished so it breathes.

Italian Bouclé

70% wool, 30% nylon. 40,000 Martindale rubs. The kind of fabric that looks better after ten years of use.
